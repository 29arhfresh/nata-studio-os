# Music Video — Prompt Template

**Format:** Music Video (Performance + Narrative)
**Duration:** Song length (typically 2–5 minutes total, composed of 3–20s clips)
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 (cinema/YouTube), 9:16 (social/vertical edit)
**Best Models:** Kling (performance consistency), Higgsfield (lip sync), Runway (style)

---

## Music Video Anatomy

### Classic Structure

| Section | Visual Approach |
|---|---|
| Intro | Establishing shots, mood setting, slow reveal |
| Verse 1 | Narrative setup, world-building, character introduction |
| Pre-Chorus | Rising tension, intercut narrative and performance |
| Chorus 1 | High-energy performance, visual peak, colour pop |
| Verse 2 | Narrative development, new locations or angles |
| Pre-Chorus 2 | Escalating intercuts, emotional build |
| Chorus 2 | Same as Chorus 1 or elevated — new angle, new action |
| Bridge | Most visually unexpected or abstract moment |
| Final Chorus | Maximum energy, all elements combined |
| Outro | Resolution, cool-down, fade |

## Pre-Production Workflow

### Step 1 — Track Analysis

Analyse the track before writing any prompts:

```
Track: [TITLE] — [ARTIST]
BPM: [X]
Time signature: [4/4, 3/4, etc.]
Total duration: [Xs]
Key sections:
  - Intro: 0:00–0:15
  - Verse 1: 0:15–0:45
  - Pre-Chorus: 0:45–0:55
  - Chorus: 0:55–1:25
  - [continue]

Emotional arc:
  - Intro: [tone]
  - Verse: [tone]
  - Chorus: [tone — usually escalation]
  - Bridge: [contrast tone]
  - Outro: [resolution tone]
```

### Step 2 — Visual Concept

Choose 2–3 visual motifs that will run through the entire video:

- **Recurring location** (e.g. rooftop, forest, black studio)
- **Recurring colour palette** (e.g. red + black, desert amber + turquoise)
- **Recurring visual element** (e.g. water, mirrors, fire, neon)

### Step 3 — Shot Map

Create a shot map before writing prompts:

| Timestamp | Section | Shot | Scene | Duration | Model |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | Intro | EWS | City skyline, dusk | 8s | Seedance |
| 0:08 | Intro | CU | Artist face, eyes closed | 4s | Kling |
| 0:15 | Verse 1 | MS | Artist walks alley | 6s | Kling |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

## Performance Shot Templates

Performance shots show the artist performing the song — lip sync, instrument play, or dance.

### Lip Sync Performance

```
[CHARACTER DESCRIPTION] performs for camera, lip sync to the track.
Direct camera address. [EXPRESSION AND ENERGY MATCHING SONG SECTION].
Camera: [MOVEMENT], [LENS] lens, [ANGLE].
Lighting: [STAGE LIGHTING DESCRIPTION].
Environment: [PERFORMANCE ENVIRONMENT].
Natural lip movement, mouth visible and clear.
```

**Example (Chorus, high energy):**
```
Woman, late 20s, black braided hair, white fitted jacket performs for camera, 
lip sync to the chorus. Intense, euphoric expression. Arms outstretched.
Camera: slow push-in, 50mm lens, eye-level.
Lighting: split red and blue stage lights, smoke machine haze, backlight rim.
Environment: empty industrial warehouse, dark, single spotlight.
Natural lip movement, mouth visible and clear.
```

**Example (Verse, intimate):**
```
Same woman, lip sync to verse. Introspective, slightly downward gaze, 
then looks up to camera. Quiet confidence.
Camera: static, 85mm lens, slight low-angle.
Lighting: single soft key from camera right, deep shadow on opposite side, warm 3200K.
Environment: same warehouse, tighter crop.
```

### Instrument Performance

```
[CHARACTER DESCRIPTION] plays [INSTRUMENT] with [ENERGY AND EXPRESSION].
Hands and instrument visible, face visible in frame.
Camera: [MOVEMENT — intercut between face and hands], [LENS].
Lighting: [DESCRIPTION].
```

### Dance / Movement Performance

```
[CHARACTER DESCRIPTION] dances [STYLE] to [ENERGY LEVEL] music.
[SPECIFIC MOVEMENTS — if key to the routine].
Camera: [MOVEMENT matching the dance energy], [LENS] lens, [ANGLE].
Lighting: [DYNAMIC STAGE LIGHTING — changes with movement].
```

## Narrative Shot Templates

Narrative shots tell the story suggested by the lyrics. They intercut with performance.

### Story Establishing Shot

```
[ENVIRONMENT] — [TIME OF DAY, WEATHER, ATMOSPHERE].
[CHARACTER] enters frame or is revealed [HOW].
Camera: [EWS OR WS], [WIDE LENS], [ANGLE].
Lighting: [ATMOSPHERIC DESCRIPTION].
[VISUAL MOTIF ELEMENT].
```

### Emotional Story Beat

```
[CHARACTER] [ACTION THAT REPRESENTS THE EMOTIONAL BEAT].
[PHYSICAL EXPRESSION OF THE EMOTION — body language, face].
Camera: [CU OR MCU], [85–135MM], [ANGLE].
Lighting: [EMOTIONALLY MOTIVATED].
```

### Visual Metaphor Shot

```
[ABSTRACT OR SYMBOLIC VISUAL] representing [LYRICAL THEME].
[HOW IT MOVES, CHANGES, OR IS REVEALED].
Camera: [MOVEMENT], [LENS], [ANGLE].
Lighting: [DESCRIPTION].
[COLOUR PALETTE — specific to this metaphor moment].
```

**Example (for a song about loss):**
```
A burning photograph in an ashtray, edges curling as it catches flame.
The image on the photo is soft and out of focus.
Camera: static, 100mm macro, slight high-angle.
Lighting: fire light only, dramatic chiaroscuro, deep black background.
Colour palette: warm amber flame, charcoal grey, cold black.
```

## Colour Strategy for Music Videos

### Section-Based Colour

Map emotional sections to colour temperatures:

| Section | Emotional Tone | Colour Temperature | Grade |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intro | Mystery, anticipation | Cool 6500K | Desaturated, teal tint |
| Verse | Narrative, grounded | Neutral 5000K | Natural, slight warmth |
| Chorus | Release, energy | Warm 3200K or bold primary | High saturation, contrast |
| Bridge | Contrast, unexpected | Opposite of chorus | Invert the grade |
| Outro | Resolution | Match intro or warmer | Return to neutral or warm |

### Visual Motif Colour

Pick a hero colour for each recurring visual element:

```
[MOTIF ELEMENT] is always lit with [COLOUR].
The hero colour [COLOUR HEX or NAME] appears in [WHERE] throughout the video.
```

## Beat Sync Principles

### Cut Timing

- **On the kick drum:** Hard cut on beat 1 of every bar.
- **On the snare:** Cut on beats 2 and 4 for double-time energy.
- **On melody phrases:** Cut at the end of 4-bar or 8-bar phrases.
- **Lyric match:** Cut on the word that matches the visual (e.g. cut to fire on "burning").

### Camera Energy to Song Energy

| Song Energy | Camera Behaviour |
|---|---|
| Low (soft verse) | Static or very slow drift |
| Building (pre-chorus) | Slow dolly-in |
| Peak (chorus) | Faster movement, tracking |
| Drop (post-chorus) | Sudden static after movement |
| Bridge (contrast) | Unexpected angle or movement |

## Lighting Design for Performance

### Stage Lighting Archetypes

| Look | Description | Mood |
|---|---|---|
| Rock / Noir | Hard backlight, dark fill, single key | Drama, grit |
| Pop / Bright | High-key, multi-colour, even fill | Joy, energy |
| R&B / Soul | Warm key, deep shadow, practical glows | Intimacy, sensuality |
| Electronic / EDM | Strobe-safe gels, LEDs, smoke | Euphoria, disorientation |
| Cinematic | Single motivated source, natural falloff | Film quality, narrative |
| Minimal | Soft frontal, clean background | Focus on the artist |

## Character Consistency in Music Videos

For artist-centric videos:

1. Write the full character clause once and use it in every prompt verbatim.
2. Use Kling with character seed for performance shots.
3. Use Higgsfield for lip sync performance shots.
4. Keep the costume identical across all shots (describe every garment detail).
5. Generate a character consistency test before the full production run.

See also: `consistent-character.md`

---

## Quality Gate for Music Videos

- [ ] Shot map covers 100% of the song duration.
- [ ] Artist character description is identical in every prompt.
- [ ] Lip sync shots use Higgsfield or Runway Act-One.
- [ ] Colour strategy is documented per section.
- [ ] Camera energy matches song energy in each section.
- [ ] Beat sync cut points are identified and planned.
- [ ] Visual motifs appear in intro, at least one chorus, and outro.
- [ ] All clips are within target model's maximum duration.
