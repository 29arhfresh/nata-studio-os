# Cinematic Sequences — Prompt Template

**Format:** Cinematic Film / Narrative Video
**Aspect Ratio:** 2.39:1 (anamorphic widescreen) or 16:9 (HD cinema)
**Duration:** Variable (3–20s per shot)
**Best Models:** Veo (image quality), Seedance 2 (physics), Kling (character)

---

## What Makes a Shot Cinematic

Cinematic is not a style — it is a set of specific technical and compositional choices:

1. **Shallow depth of field** — subject separated from background (85mm+ or open aperture).
2. **Motivated lighting** — every light source has a story reason.
3. **Controlled colour palette** — 2–3 dominant colours, not arbitrary.
4. **Intentional camera movement** — movement serves the story beat.
5. **Deliberate composition** — rule of thirds, leading lines, visual weight.
6. **Correct exposure** — controlled highlights, visible shadow detail.
7. **Film-like temporal quality** — 24fps feel, natural motion blur.

---

## Cinematic Prompt Structure

```
[SCENE DESCRIPTION — location, time, character, action].
Camera: [MOVEMENT], [LENS] lens, [ANGLE], shallow depth of field.
Lighting: [MOTIVATED SOURCE], [QUALITY], [DIRECTION], [COLOUR TEMPERATURE].
Colour palette: [2–3 DOMINANT COLOURS].
[FILM STOCK OR GRADE].
Cinematic, 24fps, film quality.
```

## Scene Type Templates

### 1. Establishing Shot

Sets location, time of day, and scale. Always the first shot of a new scene.

```
Establishing shot: [LOCATION] at [TIME OF DAY] — [WEATHER AND ATMOSPHERE].
[CHARACTER or absence of character].
Camera: wide shot, [14–35mm] lens, [ANGLE — often low or eye-level].
Lighting: [NATURAL ATMOSPHERIC — golden hour, overcast, night].
Colour palette: [PALETTE].
Deep depth of field, environment prominent.
```

**Example:**
```
Establishing shot: crumbling factory district at blue hour — overcast sky, smoke stacks.
No characters visible. Empty streets.
Camera: wide shot, 24mm lens, low-angle.
Lighting: blue hour natural light, 7000K, soft and directionless, distant industrial lights warm.
Colour palette: deep blue, steel grey, warm amber point lights.
Deep depth of field, environment prominent.
Cinematic, 24fps.
```

### 2. Master Shot

Wide coverage of the full scene geometry. Establishes spatial relationships.

```
Master shot: [FULL SCENE — all characters and their positions, what is happening].
Camera: wide shot or medium wide, [35–50mm] lens, eye-level.
Lighting: [FULL SCENE LIGHTING — all sources visible and motivated].
Colour palette: [PALETTE].
Shallow depth of field — [FOCUS SUBJECT] in focus, [BACKGROUND] soft.
```

### 3. Two-Shot (Dialogue / Confrontation)

Two characters sharing the frame.

```
Two-shot: [CHARACTER 1] and [CHARACTER 2] — [RELATIONSHIP / POWER DYNAMIC].
[POSITIONING — one foreground, one background / side by side / facing each other].
Camera: medium shot, [50–85mm] lens, eye-level.
Lighting: [KEY ON DOMINANT CHARACTER], [FILL FOR SECONDARY], [RIM FOR DEPTH].
[EMOTIONAL SUBTEXT IN THE FRAMING — who is dominant, who is vulnerable].
```

**Example:**
```
Two-shot: detective across the table from suspect — tension, interrogation.
Detective foreground left, suspect background right — power imbalance.
Camera: medium shot, 85mm lens, eye-level, slightly above suspect.
Lighting: hard key from above on detective, harsh shadow on suspect, single practical overhead bulb.
Cinematic, 24fps.
```

### 4. Over-the-Shoulder (OTS) Dialogue

Coverage of one character from behind the other's shoulder.

```
Over-the-shoulder shot: [FOREGROUND CHARACTER — back, shoulder visible] 
facing [FEATURED CHARACTER — facing camera, fully lit].
[FEATURED CHARACTER DESCRIPTION, EXPRESSION, ACTION].
Camera: medium close-up, [85mm] lens, eye-level.
Lighting: [KEY ON FEATURED CHARACTER], rim on foreground shoulder for depth separation.
```

### 5. Close-Up (Emotional Beat)

Reveals emotion. Used for key dramatic moments.

```
Close-up: [CHARACTER] — [SPECIFIC FACIAL EXPRESSION AND MICRO-EXPRESSION].
[SUBTLE MOVEMENT — eye shift, jaw set, lip tremble].
Camera: static or imperceptible drift, [85–135mm] lens, eye-level.
Lighting: [SOFT MOTIVATED LIGHT — emotional quality matters most here].
Extremely shallow depth of field. Eyes in sharp focus.
```

**Example:**
```
Close-up: woman realises she has made a terrible mistake — recognition, then dread.
Eyes widen, then jaw sets. A single controlled breath.
Camera: static, 100mm lens, eye-level.
Lighting: soft window light from camera right, shadow falls across left half of face, 5500K.
Extremely shallow depth of field. Eyes in sharp focus.
Cinematic, 24fps.
```

### 6. Insert Shot (Object / Detail)

Focus on a specific object or environmental detail that carries narrative weight.

```
Insert shot: [OBJECT] — [WHY IT MATTERS TO THE STORY].
[HOW IT IS POSITIONED, ITS CONDITION, ANY MOVEMENT].
Camera: static, [MACRO OR TELEPHOTO] lens, [SLIGHT ANGLE FOR DEPTH].
Lighting: [MOTIVATED — where does the light come from in this scene?].
Extreme close-up, shallow depth of field.
```

**Example:**
```
Insert shot: a wedding ring on the bathroom counter — abandoned, significance clear.
Ring sits in a small pool of water near the drain.
Camera: static, 100mm macro, slight high-angle.
Lighting: cool bathroom light from above, single warm practical lamp from background, 4500K.
Extreme close-up, shallow depth of field. Ring in sharp focus.
```

### 7. Tracking Shot (Character in Motion)

Camera follows a moving character.

```
Tracking shot: [CHARACTER] [MOVING ACTION — walking, running, striding].
[DIRECTION OF TRAVEL, ENVIRONMENT].
Camera: tracking [BEHIND / BESIDE / IN FRONT], [50mm] lens, eye-level.
Lighting: [CONSISTENT WITH ENVIRONMENT — natural or practical].
Natural motion, real pace. [EMOTIONAL QUALITY OF THE MOVEMENT].
```

### 8. Crane / Aerial Shot (Scale / God's Eye)

Establishes scale, power, or isolation.

```
Crane [UP / DOWN]: reveals [WHAT IS REVEALED AS CAMERA MOVES].
[CHARACTER positioned — how they relate to the scale of the environment].
Camera: crane movement, [WIDE LENS — 24mm], [ANGLE].
Lighting: [ATMOSPHERIC — appropriate to scale].
Deep depth of field, environment and character both in focus.
```

## Lighting for Cinema

### Three-Point Lighting (Interior)

```
Three-point lighting setup:
Key light: [POSITION — camera left/right, height], [QUALITY — hard/soft], [COLOUR TEMPERATURE].
Fill light: [POSITION — opposite of key], [QUALITY — softer than key], [RATIO — 2:1, 4:1].
Rim/hair light: [FROM BEHIND SUBJECT], [QUALITY], separating subject from background.
Background light: [IF APPLICABLE].
```

### Natural / Location Lighting (Exterior)

| Time | Light Quality | Colour Temp | Shadow |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sunrise | Very warm, low angle | 2000–2500K | Long, dramatic, deep orange |
| Golden Hour | Warm, directional, soft | 2500–3500K | Long, warm, soft-edged |
| Mid Morning | Neutral, climbing sun | 4000–5000K | Medium length, defined |
| Noon | Hard, overhead | 5500–6000K | Short, harsh, unflattering |
| Afternoon | Neutral to cooling | 5000–5500K | Medium, defined |
| Golden Hour PM | Same as AM golden hour | 2500–3500K | Long, warm, soft-edged |
| Blue Hour | Cool blue, soft | 7000–10000K | Almost no shadow |
| Night | Ambient only + practicals | Mixed | Deep, pool light |

## Colour Palettes by Genre

| Genre | Palette | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Thriller | Teal + grey + cold white | Desaturated, clinical, high contrast |
| Romance | Warm amber + soft gold + cream | Warm, soft, lifted blacks |
| Horror | Deep green + black + blood red | Underexposed, unnatural colour |
| Drama | Neutral + one accent colour | Natural base, emotionally motivated accent |
| Period Piece | Amber + brown + muted tones | Warm, desaturated, aged |
| Sci-Fi | Blue + teal + steel white | Cold, clinical, neon accents |
| Western | Amber + ochre + sky blue | High contrast, dust, sun-bleached |

## Cinematic Quality Keywords

Include these in every cinematic prompt:

- `shallow depth of field`
- `cinematic, 24fps`
- `film grain`
- `motivated lighting`
- `anamorphic lens flare` (use sparingly — can cause artifacts)
- `2.39:1 aspect ratio` or `16:9 letterbox`
- `[FILM STOCK] film stock`

---

## Quality Gate for Cinematic Sequences

- [ ] Every shot has a motivated light source.
- [ ] Depth of field is appropriate to focal length specified.
- [ ] Colour palette is consistent within the scene.
- [ ] Camera movement serves the story beat, not decoration.
- [ ] Spatial continuity is maintained (180-degree rule observed).
- [ ] Shot sizes vary: WS → MS → CU progression.
- [ ] Character expression matches the scene's emotional beat.
- [ ] All clips are at 24fps or the prompt specifies the frame rate intention.
