# Template — Product Commercial

A product commercial template for generating high-conversion, brand-quality video content for physical and digital products. Covers direct product showcase, lifestyle integration, and brand story formats.

---

## Commercial Video Formats

| Format | Duration | Use Case | Platform |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-roll ad | 6–15s | Forced-view paid ad | YouTube, programmatic |
| Social short | 15–30s | Engagement, organic reach | Instagram, TikTok, Reels |
| Hero brand film | 60–90s | Brand awareness, landing page | Website, YouTube |
| Product reveal | 4–8s | Product launch, CTA | All platforms |
| Testimonial | 30–60s | Trust building, conversion | Website, ads |
| Tutorial / Demo | 30–120s | Consideration, retention | YouTube, product page |
| Loop / ambient | 3–8s | Background, hero section | Website, digital signage |

---

## The Three Commercial Structures

### Structure 1: Product-First (Show, Don't Tell)
Lead with the product. Beauty shot, material quality, functional reveal.

```
BEAT 1 (0–2s): Hero product shot — maximum visual impact
BEAT 2 (2–5s): Product in action — demonstrating key benefit
BEAT 3 (5–8s): Result / transformation — what the product achieves
```

### Structure 2: Problem-Solution
Lead with the human pain point, solve it with the product.

```
BEAT 1 (0–3s): Relatable frustration or need
BEAT 2 (3–8s): Product introduction as the answer
BEAT 3 (8–12s): Resolution — person with product, satisfied
```

### Structure 3: Aspiration / Lifestyle
The product is part of a life worth wanting.

```
BEAT 1 (0–4s): Aspirational moment — character living the ideal
BEAT 2 (4–8s): Product naturally integrated into that world
BEAT 3 (8–12s): The feeling of belonging to that life
```

## Shot Hierarchy for Product Video

For a complete product video, generate these shots:

### 1. Hero Shot (Mandatory)
The signature image. Everything else supports it.

```
[SHOT SIZE — ECU to MCU]. [PRODUCT NAME/CATEGORY] centered and perfectly lit. 
[MATERIAL DESCRIPTION — texture, surface quality, finish]. 
[LIGHT QUALITY — catches [SPECIFIC MATERIAL FEATURE] beautifully]. 
Camera [BARELY PERCEPTIBLE ORBIT or STATIC]. 
[BACKGROUND — clean minimal or contextually relevant]. 
Premium, editorial, aspirational.
```

**Example — Skincare**:
```
Extreme close-up, camera very slowly orbits left to right. A matte glass serum bottle 
with a gold dropper cap sits on white Carrara marble. Morning light from the left catches 
the curved glass surface, creating a single clean highlight line. The liquid inside 
has a warm amber quality visible through the glass. Minimal, spa-like, premium.
```

---

### 2. Material / Texture Shot

```
Macro close-up, camera static. [SPECIFIC MATERIAL DETAIL — surface, texture, finish, 
mechanism, ingredient]. [LIGHT CATCHING THE MATERIAL — revealing its quality]. 
Extremely shallow depth of field, critical sharpness on [SPECIFIC POINT]. 
Slow, contemplative, the kind of detail that earns trust.
```

**Examples**:
```
// Leather product:
Macro close-up. The grain of full-grain leather catches raking sidelight, revealing 
its natural texture and hand-stitched edge detail. Shallow depth of field.

// Watch:
Macro close-up. A mechanical movement seen through the sapphire crystal caseback, 
the tiny parts oscillating in real time. Studio lighting reveals the finishing on 
each bridge and wheel. Critical focus on the balance wheel.

// Food/beverage:
Macro close-up. A single coffee bean rests on a dark surface, raking sidelight 
picking up every surface detail — the crease, the roast oil sheen. 
Studio light. Tactile, premium.
```

---

### 3. Lifestyle Context Shot

```
[SHOT SIZE — WS to MCU]. [IDEALIZED USER — see character seed approach] 
[USES / HOLDS / INTERACTS WITH PRODUCT] in [ASPIRATIONAL SETTING]. 
The product is VISIBLE and LEGIBLE but not the literal only focus — 
the FEELING is the focus. [LIGHTING]. [ATMOSPHERE].
```

**Example — Running Shoes**:
```
Medium wide shot. A woman in her late 20s, athletic build, runs on a coastal path 
at golden hour, her form effortless and fluid. Our shoes are clearly visible, 
her stride confident. The Pacific glows behind her. Camera tracks with her at a 
steadicam pace, feeling the momentum. The brand promise is the feeling: free.
```

---

### 4. Product Action / Function Shot

```
[SHOT SIZE]. [SPECIFIC FUNCTION OR TRANSFORMATION BEING DEMONSTRATED]. 
[HOW THE PRODUCT ACHIEVES IT — mechanism, formula, design feature]. 
[SENSORY LANGUAGE — how it looks, moves, sounds, feels]. 
Camera [MOVEMENT THAT ENHANCES THE ACTION]. Slow enough to appreciate every detail.
```

**Examples**:
```
// Blender:
Close-up. A powerful blender pitcher fills with colorful fruit pieces, 
then the blade activates — a vortex forms instantly, the fruits disappearing 
into a smooth green liquid in seconds. Camera holds static. The power is visceral.

// Foundation:
Close-up. A makeup brush sweeps foundation across the back of a hand in one smooth 
stroke, blending seamlessly, leaving a flawless even finish. Warm studio light 
reveals the skin-like texture of the coverage.

// Knife:
Close-up, camera static. A chef's knife slices through a tomato with zero resistance, 
the blade cutting so cleanly that the tomato holds its shape until it falls apart 
gently. Sidelight catches the edge.
```

---

### 5. Result / Transformation Shot

Show the outcome, not just the product.

```
[SHOT SIZE]. [RESULT — what the user's life or world looks like AFTER using the product]. 
[THIS SHOT SHOULD FEEL DIFFERENT FROM THE OPENING — lighter, more confident, resolved]. 
[CHARACTER if applicable, in SATISFIED or TRANSFORMED state]. 
[LIGHTING that is BRIGHTER, WARMER, or MORE EXPANSIVE than the opening shots].
```

## Prompting by Product Category

### Beauty & Skincare
```
Key priorities: skin quality, texture rendering, material of packaging, softness
Lighting: soft diffused natural light or studio beauty light
Backgrounds: white marble, warm neutral, bathroom/spa environments
Quality triggers: "dewy," "luminous," "silky," "premium formulation"
Negative: harsh shadows, clinical, cold, medical, sterile
```

### Fashion & Accessories
```
Key priorities: fabric behavior, color accuracy, wearability, aspirational lifestyle
Lighting: natural window light or fashion editorial lighting
Backgrounds: architectural, urban, lifestyle environments
Quality triggers: "editorial," "fashion-forward," "impeccable tailoring," "drape"
Negative: flat lighting, catalog quality, staged pose
```

### Food & Beverage
```
Key priorities: color vibrancy, texture and steam, freshness cues, appetite appeal
Lighting: warm window light from above-left, hero lighting for key shots
Backgrounds: natural wood, stone, kitchen surfaces
Quality triggers: "appetizing," "fresh," "artisanal," "steam rising," "glossy," "caramelized"
Negative: artificial, cold lighting, flat, underexposed, oversaturated
```

### Technology & Electronics
```
Key priorities: clean lines, screen content (add in post), material quality, precision
Lighting: controlled studio, gradient background, subtle reflections
Backgrounds: minimal dark or white, architectural context for lifestyle
Quality triggers: "precision engineering," "flawless," "minimal," "premium material," "clean"
Negative: cluttered, lifestyle-casual, worn, vintage, complex backgrounds
```

### Home Goods & Furniture
```
Key priorities: scale/proportion, material quality, lifestyle context, livability
Lighting: warm natural interior light, lifestyle home environment
Backgrounds: styled room settings, architectural environments
Quality triggers: "beautifully crafted," "at home," "lived-in luxury," "material quality"
Negative: showroom stiff, clinical, empty room, flat catalog
```

### Wellness & Health
```
Key priorities: clean, calm, human benefit, natural ingredients
Lighting: natural, soft, morning light aesthetic
Backgrounds: natural textures, white, spa-like
Quality triggers: "natural," "pure," "calming," "holistic," "balance"
Negative: pharmaceutical, clinical, hospital-adjacent, harsh, synthetic
```

## Multi-Platform Version Planning

One production; multiple aspect ratios:

| Deliverable | Aspect Ratio | Duration | Primary Motion |
|---|---|---|---|
| YouTube pre-roll (skippable) | 16:9 | 15s | Front-load the hook in first 5s |
| Instagram Feed | 1:1 or 4:5 | 15–30s | Safe zone composition |
| Instagram Reels / TikTok | 9:16 | 15–30s | Vertical first composition |
| Website hero loop | 16:9 | 4–8s seamless loop | Slow ambient motion |
| OOH digital signage | Various | 6–10s | No audio; motion tells the story |

**Tip**: When generating, produce the 9:16 version first. Most social engagement is now vertical. Reframe to 16:9 for horizontal delivery rather than starting horizontal and cropping.

## Brand Language Integration

Before prompting, define the brand's visual language:

```
BRAND VISUAL BRIEF
Brand name: [NAME]
Core feeling: [What the brand wants people to feel — 3 words max]
Color palette: [Primary and secondary brand colors]
Lighting character: [Warm/cool, hard/soft, natural/studio]
Talent type: [Describe the brand's typical person — not specific individual]
Forbidden elements: [What is off-brand — e.g., "never show clutter," "no artificial environments"]
Competitive separation: [What makes this brand look different from competitors in the category]
```

Pass this as context before writing any prompts. Every visual decision — background choice, lighting setup, camera movement speed — should be filtered through the brand brief.

## Quality Checklist for Commercial Video

- [ ] Product is clearly identifiable in the hero shot
- [ ] Material/texture quality is premium and accurate
- [ ] Lighting flatters the product and feels intentional
- [ ] Background supports rather than competes with the product
- [ ] Camera movement adds dynamism without distracting
- [ ] Lifestyle shots show the product in use, not just on display
- [ ] Aspect ratio is optimized for the target platform
- [ ] Color grade is consistent with brand visual language
- [ ] Duration is appropriate for the ad format
- [ ] No watermarks, artifacts, or anatomical errors in human shots
- [ ] Text, logo, and CTA will be added in post (not baked into AI video)
