### Color Usage Rules

1. **Primary** dominates all compositions. It governs backgrounds, primary typography, and dominant surfaces. It never competes with the accent.
2. **Accent** appears at maximum 20% of visual area per composition. It marks the single most important element.
3. **Neutral** provides visual rest. It separates primary from accent and prevents visual compression.
4. **Prohibited combinations**: [e.g. Primary on accent without neutral separation / Accent as background]

### Type Scale

| Level | Role | Size (desktop) | Weight | Tracking | Leading |
|-------|------|---------------|--------|----------|---------|
| Display | Campaign headline | [e.g. 96px] | [e.g. Regular] | [e.g. –30] | [e.g. 1.0×] |

| H2 | Sub-section headline | [e.g. 36px] | [e.g. Light] | [e.g. –10] | [e.g. 1.1×] |
| Body | Body copy | [e.g. 18px] | [e.g. Light] | [e.g. 0] | [e.g. 1.6×] |
| Caption | Labels, captions | [e.g. 12px] | [e.g. Light] | [e.g. +60] | [e.g. 1.4×] |

### Type Rules

- Primary typeface is always set at display or headline scale. Never at body size.
- Secondary typeface handles all reading-speed text (body, navigation, UI).
- Tertiary typeface is used sparingly — editorial accents and captions only.
- No mixing of primary and secondary at the same hierarchical level.
- Avoid typographic orphans: widow control is mandatory in all layout deliverables.

## Photography Style

### Subject Direction

**Expression**: [e.g. Interior — the subject is present, not performing]  
**Gaze**: [e.g. Often away from camera; when direct, it is still, not confrontational]  
**Energy**: [e.g. Stillness and presence; no action, no narrative movement]

### Lighting Signature

**Quality**: [e.g. Soft — always diffused, never hard or flash direct]  
**Source**: [e.g. Natural window or practicals that simulate natural light]  
**Direction**: [e.g. Single source from camera left; no fill]  
**Temperature**: [e.g. 5000–5600K; never warm-shifted]  
**Shadows**: [e.g. Present but gradual; no graphic black shadow zones]

### Retouching Philosophy

[e.g. Minimal. Skin texture, pores, and natural variation are preserved. Color correction to match the palette. No smoothing, no body alteration, no compositing of elements that did not exist on set.]

### Forbidden Photographic Patterns

- [e.g. Warm or golden filters applied in post]
- [e.g. Multiple light sources creating inconsistent shadow directions]
- [e.g. Heavy vignetting]
- [e.g. Oversaturated color — maximum global saturation increase: +15]
