# Creative Director

The creative decision-making system of Nata Studio OS.

---

## What This Skill Does

Creative Director is the strategic and aesthetic brain behind every Nata Studio OS output. It translates brand values, business objectives, and creative intent into concrete visual decisions — and maintains creative consistency across images, video, prompts, and copy.

This Skill covers the full creative leadership pipeline:

- **Creative strategy**: Objective translation, audience insight, storytelling angle, campaign architecture
- **Visual direction**: Composition rules, lighting philosophy, color strategy, typography guidance
- **Brand consistency**: Mandatory elements, forbidden patterns, style lock across all deliverables
- **Concept development**: Narrative arc, emotional anchor, visual metaphor
- **Moodboard creation**: Color systems, texture libraries, reference categorisation, format specs
- **Art direction**: Per-deliverable technical specification tied to the creative brief
- **Creative quality scoring**: Five-dimension weighted scoring with actionable recommendations

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use Creative Director when you need to:

- Open a new campaign, project, or brand with a structured creative brief
- Define the visual language before any image, video, or copy is produced
- Build a moodboard that communicates visual intent to a production team
- Write art direction for a specific deliverable (hero image, reel, video, copy)
- Score completed creative work against brand and quality standards
- Resolve inconsistency between deliverables by re-anchoring to the source brief
- Develop or audit a color strategy, typography system, or composition principle

## Quick Start

1. Use `templates/creative-brief.md` to capture brand, objective, audience, and tone
2. Run `buildCreativeBrief()` to generate color strategy, typography guidance, and storytelling angle
3. Build a moodboard with `buildMoodboard()` to align the team on visual language
4. Write art direction per deliverable using `buildArtDirection()`
5. Score completed work with `scoreCreative()` before handing off to production
6. Iterate on any deliverable scoring below 75 before approval

## Core Principles

**Strategy before aesthetics.** The visual decisions of a campaign derive from business objectives and audience truth — not from what looks good in isolation. The brief comes first, always.

**Specificity is direction.** "Minimal and sophisticated" is not art direction. Art direction names the exact color, the exact typeface, the exact composition rule, the exact lighting setup. Vagueness is a failure mode.

**Consistency is a system, not a rule.** Brand consistency emerges from a shared visual system — color palette, typography hierarchy, composition principle, lighting philosophy — applied across every deliverable. Enforce the system, not individual choices.

**Scoring is not judgment, it is navigation.** The five-dimension quality score identifies which creative dimension needs attention. A score below 75 triggers revision; it does not reject the work permanently.

**References are vocabulary, not instructions.** Citing Celine SS23 communicates a visual register — restraint, quality, silence. It does not mean reproducing that campaign. References establish the aesthetic conversation; the work must advance it.
