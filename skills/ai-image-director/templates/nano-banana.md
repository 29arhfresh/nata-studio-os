# Template — Nano Banana

Nano Banana is an AI image generation platform optimized for stylized, high-concept, and experimental visual content. Use Nano Banana when the brief calls for distinctive, non-standard aesthetics that fall outside the photographic realism paradigm of Flux or the painterly editorial quality of Midjourney.

---

## When to Use Nano Banana

Use Nano Banana when:
- The brief explicitly calls for stylized, graphic, or experimental aesthetics
- The image should not look like a photograph or traditional illustration
- You need high-concept visual content for editorial, music, or entertainment contexts
- The platform's distinctive stylization is the desired output quality
- Rapid stylistic exploration across unconventional aesthetics is needed

Do NOT use Nano Banana when:
- Photographic realism is required (Flux is better)
- The client needs predictable, conservative output
- Commercial accuracy is more important than visual distinctiveness
- The brief requires a standard advertising or product photography aesthetic

---

## Nano Banana Aesthetic Characteristics

Nano Banana's output tends toward:

- **High graphic contrast** — strong value relationships, bold light and shadow
- **Stylized materials** — surfaces that look rendered and designed rather than photographed
- **Color intensity** — saturated, decisive color palettes
- **Experimental scale and composition** — less bound by photographic conventions
- **Contemporary digital art sensibility** — often feels closer to digital illustration, concept art, or graphic design than photography

---

## Prompt Approach on Nano Banana

Nano Banana responds to a hybrid prompt style: concrete subject description combined with explicit style and aesthetic direction.

**What works**:
- Clear subject with distinctive visual character
- Strong color palette direction
- Art direction references from contemporary digital art, design, and culture
- Explicit mood and atmosphere instructions
- Surreal or non-realistic elements welcome

**What to avoid**:
- Photography-style technical specifications (these don't map well to Nano Banana's rendering pipeline)
- Expecting photographic material accuracy
- Over-specifying for a literal representational outcome

---

## Prompt Structure

```
[SUBJECT — described with visual character and distinctive elements]
[STYLE AND AESTHETIC — the visual language this image should speak]
[ATMOSPHERE AND MOOD — what it should feel and communicate]
[COLOR PALETTE — decisive and specific]
[COMPOSITION AND FORMAT]
[QUALITY ANCHORS]
```

## Use Cases

### High-Concept Editorial

For editorial contexts where the image needs to be visually distinctive and stop-scroll:

```
High-concept editorial image, [SUBJECT WITH UNUSUAL FRAMING OR CONTEXT].

[SURREAL OR UNEXPECTED ELEMENT — what makes this image not a conventional photograph].

[STRONG ATMOSPHERE — what the image feels like in emotional and sensory terms].

Color palette: [DECISIVE PALETTE — two to four specific colors].

Contemporary [STYLE REFERENCE — art / design / music / culture], 
editorial quality, ultra-high resolution.
```

**Example**:
```
High-concept editorial image, a young woman in a white structured suit 
floating in a void of deep navy blue, surrounded by slowly drifting 
oversized geometric shapes in pale gold and cream, 
completely still expression, direct gaze.

The space around her is impossible — dark as a studio void but 
with the quality of deep water, shapes move as if weightless.

Atmosphere: cold, precise, otherworldly, elegant.

Color palette: deep navy blue, pure white, pale gold, 
no other colors.

Contemporary luxury editorial, fashion-forward, 
AnOther Magazine or System Magazine aesthetic, 
ultra-high resolution, digital art quality.
```

### Music and Entertainment

For album art, artist imagery, and entertainment brand content:

```
[ARTIST VISUAL CONTEXT — genre, mood, positioning].

[VISUAL CONCEPT — what the image shows and implies].

[AESTHETIC LANGUAGE — the visual tradition this image speaks to].

[COLOR AND PALETTE].

Album art quality, [GENRE] aesthetic, contemporary digital art, 
ultra-high resolution.
```

### Brand and Identity (Experimental)

For brands seeking a distinctive, non-standard visual identity:

```
Brand identity image for [BRAND POSITIONING — luxury tech / experimental beauty / etc.].

[BRAND CONCEPT VISUALIZED — what the brand's values look like as an image].

[VISUAL LANGUAGE — what design tradition, art movement, or aesthetic this speaks].

Brand palette: [COLORS]. 
[MOOD AND REGISTER — premium / disruptive / futuristic / minimal / maximalist].

Brand identity photography, [DISTINCTIVE QUALITY], 
contemporary design, ultra-high resolution.
```

### Product in Extreme Concept Context

For product images that break from conventional product photography:

```
[PRODUCT — described precisely].

Unusual context: [DESCRIBE THE UNEXPECTED VISUAL TREATMENT — 
product scaled to enormous size / product in surreal environment / 
product made of unusual material / product in impossible situation].

[ATMOSPHERE AND STYLE].

Color palette: [STRONG PALETTE].

Concept art product design, advertising campaign experimental, 
ultra-high resolution.
```

## Color Direction

Nano Banana responds strongly to decisive color palette direction.

### Palette Structures

**Monochromatic with one accent**:
```
Dominant palette: [BASE COLOR] in full range from shadow to highlight.
Single accent: [ACCENT COLOR] used sparingly for maximum impact.
```

**Complementary high contrast**:
```
Complementary palette: [COLOR A] and [COLOR B] in roughly equal balance, 
high contrast, vibrant tension between the two colors.
```

**Analogous harmony**:
```
Analogous color palette: [COLOR 1], [COLOR 2], [COLOR 3] — adjacent on the 
color wheel, harmonious, unified sense of atmosphere.
```

**Dark with neon**:
```
Dark foundation: deep [COLOR] almost to black.
Neon accent: bright [NEON COLOR] in specific elements — 
[what has the neon quality], glowing against darkness.
```

**Pale and ethereal**:
```
Pale palette: soft [COLOR 1], pale [COLOR 2], muted [COLOR 3], 
very low saturation overall with occasional accent pop.
```

## Avoiding Common Pitfalls on Nano Banana

**Do not expect photographic material accuracy**: Nano Banana is not optimized for precise surface material rendering (matching exact fabric weaves, precise glass frosting, etc.). For material-critical work, use Flux.

**Do not anchor too heavily on real-world photographic references**: References to specific photographers' work or camera specifications are less effective here. Reference art directors, designers, or art movements instead.

**Do lean into what Nano Banana does**: If your first generation looks unexpected or unusual in an interesting way, that may be Nano Banana's strength, not a failure. Evaluate against the emotional and aesthetic brief, not just literal accuracy.
