# Template — Advertising Photography

Use this template for brand campaign images, hero advertising assets, OOH (out-of-home) billboards, digital banners, and any image where the primary goal is brand communication and commercial persuasion.

---

## When to Use This Template

- Campaign hero images (print, digital, OOH)
- Brand identity photography
- Seasonal campaign assets
- Social advertising content (paid and organic)
- Product-led advertising with lifestyle or aspirational context
- Luxury and premium brand visual content
- Cause-based and values-driven brand imagery

---

## Advertising Photography Principles

**The image must communicate before it is read.** At billboard distance or on a three-second social scroll, the visual must transmit the brand message without supporting copy. Ask: what does this image say in 0.5 seconds?

**Every element is intentional.** In advertising photography, props, colors, materials, and backgrounds are not set dressing — they are brand signals. A premium brand cannot have a cheap background. A sustainability brand cannot have plastic props.

**Copy space is a design requirement.** Advertising images almost always need room for a headline, logo, and call to action. Design negative space into the composition deliberately.

## Section 2: Visual Strategy

Before writing a single prompt word, determine the visual strategy.

### Strategy Framework

**1. What is the hero?**  
The subject that must carry the most visual weight. Can be a product, a person, an environment, or an idea.

**2. What is the supporting story?**  
Context, emotion, or lifestyle that frames and elevates the hero. Should not compete with the hero.

**3. What is the brand signal?**  
The visual element that communicates brand identity regardless of logo presence — color, texture, material quality, lifestyle context, photography aesthetic.

**4. What is the emotional register?**  
The feeling the image must produce in the viewer. Desire, trust, comfort, excitement, aspiration, nostalgia, confidence, cool.

### Visual Strategy Template

```
Hero: [SUBJECT AND HOW IT DOMINATES THE FRAME]
Story: [SUPPORTING CONTEXT — environment, person, props, situation]
Brand signal: [VISUAL ELEMENT THAT COMMUNICATES BRAND IDENTITY]
Emotional register: [WHAT THE VIEWER SHOULD FEEL IN 0.5 SECONDS]
```

## Section 3: Composition for Advertising

### Copy Space Design

Advertising images need deliberate negative space. Design for it.

**Top third copy space**:
```
Subject positioned in lower two-thirds of frame, 
upper third clear sky / clean background / solid color plane, 
clean area for headline placement, no competing visual elements in top zone.
```

**Bottom strip copy space**:
```
Subject fills upper three-quarters, 
lower quarter [COLOR / dark / light] gradient fading to solid, 
clean area for logo and tagline.
```

**Side copy space**:
```
Subject on [left/right] third of frame, 
[right/left] two-thirds clean, solid, or gradient plane.
```

**Full bleed (no copy space in image)**:
```
Subject fills frame edge to edge, 
copy will overlay on image in post-production.
Note: Ensure sufficient contrast for white or dark text legibility.
```

### Compositional Power Moves in Advertising

**Center lock**: Hero product or face dead center, everything else symmetrical around it. Communicates authority, premium positioning.

**Rule of thirds off-center**: Subject on one third, negative space on other two-thirds. Creates tension and visual interest, space for copy.

**Environmental frame**: Natural or architectural elements frame the subject — a doorway, an arch, overhanging branches, an alley. Creates depth and context.

**Low angle hero**: Camera below subject looking up. Communicates power, aspiration, premium positioning. Classic luxury advertising move.

**Overhead flat lay**: Camera directly above, subject arranged below. Works for beauty, food, product campaigns. Clean and graphic.

## Section 4: Brand Tier Lighting

Lighting communicates brand tier more powerfully than any other single element.

### Luxury Tier

```
Dark, controlled, single or dual source, dramatic contrast, 
no ambient fill spill, precise specular highlights, 
deep rich shadows with visible detail, 
every surface reflects the light source crisply.
Color temperature: warm narrow range (2700K–3200K) OR extremely cool and clinical (6500K–7000K).
No mixed temperatures.
```

### Premium / Contemporary

```
Clean, directional, editorial quality, 
soft diffusion from large source, controlled fill, 
slight rim light for separation, 
background separated from subject.
Color temperature: 5000K–5500K neutral.
```

### Accessible / Mass Market

```
Bright, even, optimistic, 
high-key lighting approach, open shadows, 
accessible and approachable, no drama, no mystery.
Color temperature: 5500K–6000K bright daylight or natural morning light.
```

### Lifestyle / Brand Values

```
Natural, motivated light, 
available light feeling even if studio-lit, 
imperfect and human, 
not over-produced or over-retouched.
Color temperature: matches the environment (golden hour 3500K / office 4000K / outdoor 6000K).
```

## Section 5: Color Strategy in Advertising

### Brand Color Discipline

Advertising images must reinforce brand color codes, not fight them.

```
Brand palette: [PRIMARY BRAND COLOR], [SECONDARY], [ACCENT].
Image palette: Image color grade should complement the brand palette, not clash.
[COLOR TO AMPLIFY]: [should read as prominent in the image]
[COLOR TO SUPPRESS]: [should not appear prominently]
```

### Common Advertising Color Strategies

**Monochromatic luxury**: Background and shadows in one tonal family, product provides the accent color.

**Complementary tension**: Product in one hue, environment in the complementary hue. Creates energy and visual contrast.

**Tonal harmony**: Everything in the image in a close tonal range — greens, natural tones, earth tones, for example. Communicates harmony, naturalness, sustainability.

**White and one accent**: Pure white or near-white everything, product or subject provides a single bright accent color. Communicates cleanliness, premium, simplicity.

## Section 6: Advertising by Format

### OOH / Billboard (16:9 or 2:1)

```
Wide format composition, [COPY ZONE POSITION], 
image readable at 30 meters and 100 meters, 
simple visual hierarchy — hero subject dominant, no competing complexity, 
strong contrast between subject and background (legible at distance), 
no fine detail that reads at billboard scale, 
[ASPECT RATIO: 16:9 or 2:1 or custom].
```

### Social Square (1:1)

```
Square format composition, 
subject centered or slightly off-center, 
frame fills fully, tight and graphic, 
no empty corner space, 
all important visual elements within safe zone (80% of frame), 
high energy and visual clarity at small size.
```

### Social Story / Reel Cover (9:16)

```
Vertical format, 9:16 ratio, 
subject upper two-thirds (thumb-scroll first impression), 
bottom third [clear for text / lifestyle context / brand element], 
mobile-optimized — reads on phone screen at 375px width, 
bold visual impact, no small detail.
```

### Digital Banner (various)

```
High visual impact in small format, 
[ASPECT RATIO], 
hero element dominant and immediately readable, 
minimal environmental complexity, 
clean background with strong subject contrast.
```

## Advertising Prompt Examples by Brand Tier

### Luxury Watch Campaign

```
Luxury campaign advertising, premium watch, dark editorial.

Close-up of a mechanical watch on a male wrist, three-quarter angle, 
watch face centered, 30° tilt toward camera. 
Dark charcoal tailored suit cuff visible, crisp white shirt cuff visible beneath.

Narrow strip light from above right, 3200K warm, 
single sharp specular on watch crystal and polished case, 
deep shadow filling left and lower frame. 
No fill. No ambient.

100mm macro lens, f/8, tack sharp entire watch face, hands, and indices, 
very slight compression of background (arm).

Upper quarter clear dark gradient for headline placement.

Palette: near-black, deep charcoal, warm gold specular accent, white cuff.
Color grade: dark, muted, warm gold only, no color cast.

Luxury watch advertising, Rolex Vacheron Constantin tier, 
shot on Hasselblad medium format, ultra-high resolution, 
commercial watch photography quality.
Aspect ratio: 4:5.
```

### Sustainable Fashion Campaign

```
Brand campaign photography, sustainable apparel, values-driven.

Woman, early 30s, warm medium brown skin, natural hair in a loose bun, 
minimal makeup, wearing a soft moss green linen coat, wide-leg cream trousers, 
carrying a woven natural straw bag. Relaxed and comfortable.

Walking through an early morning farmers' market, 
fresh produce stalls visible in background, 
dappled light through market canopy, 
relaxed and natural — not styled for camera.

Natural overcast morning light, 6000K, soft and diffused, 
even illumination, slight overhead quality, 
ambient fill from surrounding market.

35mm lens, f/4.0, full body in frame with context, 
loose composition, subject not rigidly centered, 
spontaneous feeling.

Left third clear sky/canopy for headline placement.

Color palette: earth tones, moss green, cream, straw, warm natural.

Sustainable brand campaign, Patagonia / Eileen Fisher aesthetic, 
lifestyle photography, natural and real, not over-produced, 
shot on film-style, golden hour soft quality.

```
