# Template — Fashion Photography

Use this template for editorial fashion photography, lookbook images, campaign lifestyle, runway-inspired stills, and any image where garments are the primary commercial subject.

---

## When to Use This Template

- Editorial fashion for magazines, brand campaigns, and lookbooks
- E-commerce fashion where garment detail must read clearly
- Campaign lifestyle imagery for clothing and accessories brands
- Runway-inspired and fashion-forward creative direction
- Seasonal lookbook production
- Accessory and footwear editorial

---

## Fashion Photography Categories

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Editorial** | Art-directed, narrative or conceptual, mood and garment as equals |
| **Commercial editorial** | Garment-forward, editorial quality, commercially viable, no abstract concepts |
| **Lookbook** | Clean, consistent, garment-led, multiple looks across consistent aesthetic |
| **Campaign** | Brand message dominant, lifestyle or storytelling context |
| **E-commerce fashion** | Garment clarity, white or clean background, multiple angles |
| **Accessory** | Bags, shoes, jewelry as primary subject, often product + model |

## Section 2: Garment Description

The garment description is the most important section in fashion photography. Be exhaustive.

### Garment Template

```
[GARMENT TYPE]: [COLOR], [FABRIC AND TEXTURE], [FIT AND CUT], 
[DISTINCTIVE DETAILS — lapels, buttons, seams, drape, weight], 
[CONDITION — new, worn, draped, structured].
```

### Fabric Vocabulary

**Weave and structure**:
- `silk chiffon, sheer, lightweight, flowing, slight sheen`
- `heavy wool tweed, structured, slight texture, holds shape`
- `raw denim, stiff, visible selvedge, indigo weft`
- `cashmere knit, soft, slight pile visible, holds drape`
- `organza, crisp, semi-transparent, volume without weight`
- `leather, structured, slight surface variation, matte or gloss`
- `linen, natural wrinkle, loose weave, matte, breathes`
- `velvet, directional nap, rich color depth, slight sheen variation by direction`

**Garment behavior**:
- `fabric in slight motion, natural flutter`
- `garment draped and settled, no movement`
- `structured and tailored, holds clean line`
- `soft and unstructured, follows body`
- `billowing in implied breeze`

### Common Styling Directions

**Commercial clean**: `garments pressed and styled crisply, all buttons done, seams straight, professional and put-together`

**Casual lifestyle**: `naturally worn, slightly relaxed, no visible styling effort, garment looks lived-in and comfortable`

**Luxury formal**: `impeccably tailored, every detail deliberate, structured and precise, no creases, clean lines`

## Section 4: Pose and Movement

Fashion poses must show the garment's key design elements.

### Pose Decision Rules

- A garment with strong shoulders → photograph from slightly below to emphasize the shoulder line
- A dress or skirt with movement → pose to show fabric motion (mid-step, slight turn)
- A structured jacket or coat → photograph from three-quarter angle to show front, lapels, and silhouette depth
- Trousers → full body shot to show leg line and break on shoe
- An accessory → position pose to frame the accessory in a featured position
- A back design → include a back or three-quarter rear pose

### Pose Vocabulary

**Full body**: `mid-stride walking, slight turn to camera`, `standing in contrapposto, weight on right hip`, `one foot forward, hands in pockets`, `arms at sides, relaxed, looking forward`

**Three-quarter body**: `facing camera, slight lean on architectural element`, `arms loosely crossed at waist`, `hand in hair, elbow raised`

**Upper body**: `shoulders back, chin slightly lifted`, `looking down, slight movement in shoulders`, `direct gaze, confident stillness`

### Pose Template

```
[BODY POSITION — full body/three-quarter/upper], [STANCE AND WEIGHT], 
[ARM AND HAND POSITION], [HEAD ANGLE], [GAZE DIRECTION], 
[MOVEMENT — still/slight motion/mid-motion/strong motion].
```

## Section 6: Lighting for Fashion

### Editorial Fashion Lighting

**Natural outdoor — golden hour**:
```
Golden hour backlight from [DIRECTION], sun at [ANGLE] from horizon, 
warm 3800K directional light, rim effect on shoulder and hair, 
soft warm fill from open sky, ambient bounce from surroundings.
```

**Natural outdoor — overcast**:
```
Bright overcast daylight, 6500K, soft even diffused light, 
directional quality lost, wrap-around softness, ideal for 
fabric texture and skin rendering.
```

**Studio fashion — dramatic**:
```
Single large softbox from camera [DIRECTION], [ANGLE]° down, 
5500K, high contrast, strong shadow on floor and wall. 
[FILL — minimal / none / slight reflector]. 
[RIM — yes, strong / no].
```

**Studio fashion — editorial soft**:
```
Large overhead octabox, beauty dish or strip lights flanking at 45°, 
soft wraparound quality, minimal shadow fill, clean highlight on face.
5500K daylight balanced throughout.
```

**Mixed practical**:
```
[PRACTICAL SOURCE — large window / neon / fluorescent], 
supplemented by [FILL SOURCE], [COLOR TEMPERATURE RELATIONSHIP — matching / intentional contrast].
```

## Section 7: Lens and Composition for Fashion

### Focal Length for Fashion

| Focal Length | Fashion Use |
|-------------|-------------|
| 24–35mm | Environmental, full body with context, fashion reportage |
| 50mm | Natural, versatile, editorial neutral perspective |
| 85mm | Standard fashion portrait and three-quarter, flattering |
| 135mm | Fashion close-up, compression of background, magazine cover |
| 35mm at low angle | Power, makes model appear taller, dramatic full-body |

### Compositional Conventions

**Full body**: `feet visible in frame, 10% negative space above head, subject on vertical center`

**Three-quarter**: `mid-thigh to above crown, subject on left or right third, negative space for gaze`

**Portrait/beauty**: `chin to crown, slight low angle, face on center or third`

**Low angle full body**: `camera at approximately knee height looking up, model fills frame vertically, dramatic compression of background`

## Section 8: Color and Grade for Fashion

### Fashion Grade Vocabulary

**Editorial luxury**: `desaturated slightly, color palette controlled, shadow detail lifted, no blown highlights, skin tones warm, garment colors true, film quality`

**Commercial clean**: `accurate colors, no cast, skin tones natural, garment color true to life, slight brightening, maximum garment readability`

**Warm lifestyle**: `warm tonal grade, golden hour warmth, shadows amber, highlights cream, Kodak film reference`

**Cool editorial**: `cool desaturated, blue-grey shadows, skin tones slightly cool and editorial, high-fashion magazine aesthetic`

**High contrast**: `crushed shadows, bright highlights, graphic impact, high-fashion club or runway reference`

## Negative Prompt (Fashion)

```
blurry, low quality, jpeg artifacts, distorted proportions, 
deformed hands, bad anatomy, wrong number of fingers, 
garment distorted, fabric looks digital, pattern errors, 
oversaturated colors, color bleeding, unrealistic skin, 
plastic appearance, amateurish, cheap-looking, 
poor composition, cut-off limbs (unless intentional)
```

## Fashion-Specific Iteration Guide

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Garment pattern repeats incorrectly | AI struggles with complex textile patterns | Avoid describing complex patterns; use solid colors or describe pattern loosely |
| Fabric looks synthetic/digital | Missing material specificity | Add detailed fabric descriptor: weave type, weight, behavior, reflectivity |
| Model proportions distorted | Generic fashion model prompt | Add specific height impression and build description |
| Garment doesn't fit correctly | Fit description too vague | Add "fitted at [AREA], [LOOSER/TIGHTER] through [AREA], falls to [LENGTH]" |
| Background competes with garment | Setting too detailed | Add "background out of focus, shallow DOF" or simplify the background description |
